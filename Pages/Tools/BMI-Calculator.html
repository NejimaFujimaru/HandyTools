About BMI & WHO Standards

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple calculation using a person's height and weight. The formula is BMI = weight (kg) / [height (m)]2. It is widely used to categorize individuals into different weight groups and assess potential health risks.

The World Health Organization (WHO) provides the following BMI categories:

  • Underweight: BMI < 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5 – 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25 – 29.9
  • Obese: BMI ≥ 30
These standards help identify whether your weight is healthy for your height and guide you towards maintaining or achieving a healthy lifestyle.

Benefits of Good Health

  • Improved energy and mood
  • Lower risk of chronic diseases (heart disease, diabetes, etc.)
  • Better sleep and mental health
  • Enhanced physical performance

Motivation for Exercise & Healthy Lifestyle

Regular exercise and healthy habits are key to maintaining a normal BMI and overall well-being. Physical activity strengthens your body, boosts immunity, and helps manage stress. Adopting a balanced diet, staying active, and avoiding harmful habits like smoking or excessive drinking can greatly improve your quality of life.

Use your BMI as a starting point to set health goals, stay motivated, and make positive changes for a happier, healthier future!
